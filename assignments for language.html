Grammar Scavenger Hunt (T3: Language) 


Begin by browsing through the revision project discussion boards, websites, social media posts, or emails you've received (or written), looking for errors or irregularities in grammar or usage. For each one you find, consider how you would correct the error.


Please do not just google “examples of ____ error” and paste in the answers. Find your error examples in real-life, to make this assignment more authentic.


For this assignment, we’re talking about “Standard American English.”* What is or isn’t an “error” can vary depending on context, intended audience, etc. This activity is not intended as a judgment of anyone’s writing or speaking; it’s an opportunity to think like an editor. If you disagree with something someone posts in the Discussion Board, engage them but don’t yell at them.


Following are some examples of kinds of errors you may look for. You may wish to watch this video explaining several of the grammar topics involved with this assignment.

  • Find an example of a run-on sentence. Video examples!
  • Find an example of a sentence fragment.
  • Find an example of a misplaced or dangling modifier. Here’s a video discussing an example of a dangling modifier.
  • Find an example of a punctuation error.
  • Find an example of a capitalization error.
  • Find an example of a verb tense inconsistency (for example, shifting between present tense and past tense in the same bit of narrative, for no apparent reason).
  • Find an example of a pronoun error (for example, the wrong case or number of pronoun was used, or there is a pronoun reference problem). (NOTE: the linked resource for “wrong case or number” only briefly mentions the use of “they/them/their” as singular pronouns; in recent years, use of these pronouns as gender-neutral singular (or plural) has become increasingly mainstream/widely accepted. A little bit of casual research reveals that this is nothing new.)
  • Find an example of “telling” that might be more effective as “showing.” Video discussion!
  • Maybe I will come up with some kind of prize for anyone who successfully and accurately (final decision mine, lol) comes up with a post for all eight of the above! 🏆


For each error you find, make a post in the “Grammar Scavenger Hunt!” Discussion Board topic in class, showing the error and how you would correct it. Find as many as you can or want to! Shall we say, aim for at least three? If you want to get "ADVANCED” on this one, find more unique examples and/or reply to classmates’ posts if you see a different way to correct the error, or think it's not actually an error (why?), or think it's an error but not the one the person said it was.


Do I really have to say let's be civil about it, keep an attitude of curiosity and exploration, and don't take any of it personally! 😁




*Although it must be said that what “Standard American English” means, and whether or how much we should care, is a tense and controversial topic in itself.
