Many successful writers cobble their work together over an extended period of time. Taking notes, writing on schedule, doing character sketches—these are just some of the things writers do along the way towards the conclusion of a finished work.

Webster’s New International Dictionary of the English Language (1913 revised edition) defines memoir thusly:

  1. A memorial account; a history composed from personal experience and memory; an account of transactions or events (usually written in familiar style) as they are remembered by the writer. See History, 2.

  2. A memorial of any individual; a biography; often, a biography written without special regard to method and completeness.

  3. An account of something deemed noteworthy; an essay; a record of investigations of any subject; the journals and proceedings of a society.

Memory is a powerful force. I suspect “commemoration” is a fundamental drive for anyone who tends towards any type of artistry. Over the next couple of weeks, keep in mind the phrase, “I remember...” and come back to it any time you get stuck. 

See where it takes you.
