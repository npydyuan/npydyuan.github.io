I am from pavement


I am from pavement.

From the womb of the night 

and the caress of the day.


I am from a good book,

from sweet caffeine   

and a hopeful dream.

I am from the mountains

(the ones I cannot recall)

and the woods (the ones I can).


I am from my brother’s teachings,

  from the oak tree reaching

and summer’s relentless beatings.

I am from the family fireplace.

(Red, warm. 

It smelled of long-gone winters.)


I am from my favorite blanket,

and endless autumn rain

whose falling promised better plans.

I am from springtime

and car rides,

from emerald and evergreen.


I am from pen and paper,

and the hours we spent

locked in battle without rest.

I am from spelling tests,

from “Not-there-yet”s

from stuffed bears and armchairs.


My memories shall forever be draped

in satin and lace,

rusted and golden with time.

I am from my mother’s eyes,

from juvenile cries

and little white lies


– Morgan Poole
