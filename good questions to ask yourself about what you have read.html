good questions to ask yourself about what you have read

Here are some examples of questions you can respond to when asked to (for example) “Study a Mentor Text”

(Choose one or more)

  • What does the text say to you?
  • How do you interpret the author’s message for the reader regarding writing, learning, or life?
  • What literary, rhetorical, or poetic techniques do you notice?
  • What are the three most useful or compelling ideas in the text related to how might they apply to your own writing?
  • Come up with questions of your own, based on thoughts or ideas that occur to you as you read whichever mentor text you chose. Annotating as you read is a good way to come up with good questions, as is making connections.
