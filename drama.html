A writer’s relationship to drama can be challenging.

Literarydevices.net defines drama as “a composition in verse or prose presenting a story in pantomime or dialogue. It contains conflict of characters, particularly the ones who perform in front of an audience on the stage. The person who writes drama for stage directions is known as a dramatist or playwright.”

Another site (I lost the link, oh well) says it’s “great for a creative writing project” because it “offers opportunities to work on character development, story structure, and a whole other set of writing skills.” Sounds good to me!

If you wish to explore this literary form, you will be able to focus on developing and strengthening writing as needed by planning, revising, and editing—this time through the lens of the dramatist or playwright.
