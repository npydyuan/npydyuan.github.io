tinkering with language

Once you’ve perused some of the links and resources on this page, try these suggested assignments for language.


Whenever you proofread and edit (for one of your revision projects, for example) strive for clarity. Consider the following, and review as needed:

  • Avoid run-on sentences
  • Do not join independent clauses by a comma
  • Avoid sentence fragments
  • Watch out for commonly confused words
  • Find and fix apostrophe errors
  • Review punctuation rules
  • Review semicolon usage
  • Review capitalization rules
  • Find and fix pronoun reference errors
  • Find and fix pronoun agreement errors
  • Don’t mix present and past tense
  • Make your subject agree with your verb
  • Find and fix errors in parallel structure
  • Avoid dangling and misplaced modifiers
  • Avoid clichés
  • Use the active voice
  • Omit needless words
  • Use definite, specific, concrete language
  • Avoid a succession of loose sentences
  • Keep related words together
  • Place the emphatic words of a sentence at the end
  • Clear up awkward sentences
  • Put statements in positive form
