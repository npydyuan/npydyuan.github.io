a small bead of melted wax

Stop and write about “a small bead of melted wax” if you wish. Or you could go back to where the river begins.

A birthday cake is not just any other cake. No, a birthday cake is a cake only eaten on, you guessed it, birthdays; it comes in many different flavors and designs depending on what the birthday boy or girl wants. On my birthday, I had a chocolate cake with raspberry frosting. It was delicious, nobody complained.

However, believe it or not, not everyone has birthday cake on their birthday. Some people don’t even celebrate their birthdays. Even my boyfriend says he doesn’t do much for his birthday. As someone who always grew up celebrating birthdays, this was foreign to me. Why would someone not want to celebrate their birthday? Then it occurred to me that not everyone thinks of birthdays the same way I do. You see, when I think of birthdays, I see a family gathered around a dining table that’s too small for everyone to fit around. I see smiles of all kinds, some perfectly straight, some with teeth missing, some gapped. I see two candles, the flames dancing in the exhalation of air, a small bead of melted wax running down the side. I think of being surrounded by everyone I love and care about.

Birthdays are a celebration of living another year. Some people say, “So what? You lived another year, big deal!” It is a big deal. I think living another year is extraordinary. You are not guaranteed tomorrow, and it’s crazy how many people live like they are. Years come with many ups and downs. Sometimes you are left going through hardships, ones that put you in a severe depression. When it’s hard to get out of bed because every muscle in your body is telling you to stay there and wallow in your emotions. Everything is numb, and it feels as if your life doesn’t matter anymore. Millions of people go through this each year, and many don’t make it out alive. If you did, isn’t that something worth celebrating?

With the hardships comes prosperity. When you feel truly happy. There’s this buzz in your stomach that’s ready to take on whatever comes at you. You wake up smiling because you are thankful to live another day. Now, isn’t that something worth celebrating too? Celebrating your birthday is acknowledging that you lived through both the bad days and the good. Life is beautiful, and just living in it for one day should be celebrated. If you think about it, we are living on a ball of water and mud that’s spinning constantly and floating in a seemingly endless universe. What about that is not amazing? Sometimes you just need to step out of your own mind for a second and look at the entirety of the world you live in. I believe every being, whether mammal, insect, or reptile, should be able to exprience the beauty of Earth.

People often don’t like to celebrate their birthdays because they mean they are aging. So many people fear getting older because they view it as another year closer to death. Not to mention, the wrinkles, joint pain, and overall depletion of health that comes with it. We are often seen as beautiful when we are young. Older adults always envious of the age difference. “Oh to be 23 again,” they say with a hint of despair in their voice. People think the only opportunity for a good life happens within the first few decades of your life. However, this is simply not true. Many middle-aged people still haven’t found their purpose in life. I know many people in their late 40s or early 50s who are just now discovering something they love. There should be no pressure to feel like you need to find your purpose in life before you have even lived. Age is just a number, as they say. It shouldn’t shape who you are or affect you from living the life you truly want to live. Every year should be celebrated whether you be turning 28 or 82. Every exprience you’ve had, every encounter, every emotion, should all be celebrated because that’s what life is all about. It’s about the ups and the downs, the twists and turns, and everything in between.

You only get one life, and you have to live it. Live it like you’re going to die tomorrow because you might. Jump onto the life train and don’t look back no matter what you do. Everyone is going to die but how you live is up to you.
