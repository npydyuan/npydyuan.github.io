"We all get different meanings" from a poem. True, but so what? This video is a quick take on how to have something interesting to write about a text, even (especially) when there isn't just one right answer.

Because poetry involves unexpected language, it involves randomness or chance, which is a feature of play. Therefore, playing with language can lead to poetic serendipity. The thesaurus game is just one example of a way to play with words and encourage happy accidents.

Language is weird and that's what makes it magical. I would like you to meet Janusnode.
