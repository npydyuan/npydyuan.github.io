Historical and Rhetorical Context (from AVID)

Texts are not written in a vacuum; they require the reader to adequately understand the historical and rhetorical context for the reading. To fully understand a text, a reader must consider who the author is, what he or she is responding to, and what was going on during the time in which the text was written.
