Watch some (free!) short films to get inspired for writing your own script!

“For short films, finding an audience is an often uphill battle. Even major award winners struggle to reach viewers outside of the festival circuit. Thank goodness for The Screening Room, The New Yorker’s online platform for sharing short films. It’s a magnificent free buffet for those of us who’d like nothing better than to gorge ourselves on these little gems.”

You may very well have heard of the story called “The Necklace,” by Guy de Maupassant. Several authors have made one-act plays based on the original story. Check out the example(s) below, and notice the different choices made by each author. Think about some of these different ways to interpret a story, so you can make some of the same kinds of decisions when you're working on your own dramatic writing!

  • one-act play by Burton Bumgarner (what an awesome name!)
  • one-act play by Ray Smith

For a great example of a three-act play, check out Rhinoceros by Eugène Ionesco, if you want to read something from the 1950’s that seems uncannily relevant to our times. (here’s some background info from wikipedia)
